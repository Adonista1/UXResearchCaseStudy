UX Research Case Study: Zillow HBCU Hackathon

Building Trust with the Next Generation of Homebuyers

Background: Zillow is an American tech real-estate marketplace company, commonly known for being the go-to platform for connecting landlords to renters and buyers. In this project, my team and I will tackle a core problem: how to maintain relevance and consumer trust with the next wave of homebuyers.

üè† Context: My Zillow Story

Context: Zillow was my designated company to analyze and solve for in my undergraduate courses, and I also participated in the Zillow HBCU Hackathon with three of my peers allowing me to conduct a deep analysis, iterate products, and ideate marketing campaigns.

My Core Responsibility

Define the emotional user pain points and translate complex economic threats into specific product requirements and a measurable strategic plan.

The Overarching Business Goal

Counter market anxiety by establishing Zillow as an empathetic, trustworthy resource.

Key Metric Target

Drive a 15% sales/lease increase and a 10% mobile app download boost by January 2024.

üíî The Core Problem & Product Objectives

The Hackathon required us to solve three core, non-negotiable **objectives** that directly addressed the anxiety, distrust, and economic barriers faced by our target users.

PlainZight Credit Care (Unified Financial Dashboard)

Objective Fulfilled: User-Friendly Focus

The Insight: Users are overwhelmed by fragmented loan/search processes. They need a single, low-anxiety journey.

The Solution: This dashboard integrates mortgage readiness and financial education, acting as the centralized hub to continuously improve financial well-being while hunting for a home. This reduces anxiety and drives app usage.

üìà Impact & Next Steps

Product Impact I Drove

  • Problem Resolution: The PlainZight Zestimate Component immediately addressed the core brand weakness (Zestimate scrutiny), building user trust.
  • Ethical Design: Inclusion of the Fair Market Filter demonstrated proactive commitment to the mitigate bias objective, positioning Zillow as an ethical market leader.
  • Metric Validation: The unified design was estimated to drive the key goal of 10% increase in mobile app usage by reducing user anxiety.

My Recommendations Moving Forward

  • Trust Validation: Conduct follow-up usability studies on the new Zestimate Component to confirm that user trust signals have improved post-implementation.
  • Bias Testing: Develop an internal audit to test the effectiveness of the Fair Market Filter in providing equitable outcomes across diverse user demographics.

üìù Reflection: Lessons Learned

I truly enjoyed working on this project, even though we faced limited resources and several setbacks along the way. Despite these challenges, I believe our team developed an effective and well-researched strategy to address the core problems identified in our research.

The timing of the hackathon made things particularly difficult, as it coincided with exam season, which caused scheduling conflicts within the team. Coming from a non-technical background in undergrad also presented its own challenges. While I contributed heavily to the research, planning, and strategic development, there were moments when my lack of technical experience prevented me from executing certain tasks independently. This sometimes meant having to wait on others to complete pieces of the project before moving forward, which tested my patience and adaptability.

Still, this experience was incredibly rewarding. I learned about various technical tools and concepts‚Äîwhat an API is, how to use GitHub, and even got to explore design for the first time. Beyond the technical growth, I gained a deeper appreciation for teamwork, communication, and balancing strengths within a group. Overall, this project pushed me out of my comfort zone and helped me build confidence in areas I once found intimidating.
